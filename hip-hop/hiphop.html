About Hip-Hop

Hip-Hop dance emerged in the streets of New York during the 1970s as a raw and powerful form of self-expression among the youth. It evolved as a part of hip-hop culture, alongside graffiti art, DJing, MCing, and knowledge.

Known for its freestyle nature, high energy, and rhythmic movements, Hip-Hop incorporates styles like breaking, popping, locking, and krumping. Dancers often perform in battles, cyphers, and community jams — where individuality and creativity take center stage.

Today, Hip-Hop has exploded globally — from underground street performances to commercial stages, movies, and dance competitions — influencing fashion, language, and identity in every corner of the world.
