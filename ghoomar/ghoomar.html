About Ghoomar

Ghoomar is a traditional folk dance of Rajasthan, known for its graceful circular movements, vibrant attire, and rhythmic footwork. Originally performed by the **Bhil tribe** and later adopted by **Rajput women**, it has become a symbol of pride, celebration, and feminine grace in Rajasthani culture.

The dance is performed during festive occasions such as weddings, Teej, Gangaur, and Holi. Women wear flowing **ghagras (skirts)**, **odhanis (veils)**, and ornate jewelry, spinning in rhythm to folk songs sung in praise of valor, devotion, and beauty.
