About Belly Dance

Belly dance, also known as **Raqs Sharqi** in Arabic, is a captivating dance form that originated in the Middle East. It emphasizes intricate movements of the torso and hips, layered with elegant arm gestures, shimmies, and isolations.

This dance form is celebrated for its empowering nature and feminine grace. Over time, it has evolved into various regional styles — from Egyptian and Turkish to Tribal Fusion and Modern Belly Dance — each bringing unique flair and music.

Today, belly dance is a global phenomenon, taught and performed across cultures, often as a symbol of beauty, rhythm, storytelling, and self-expression.
