About Ballet

Ballet is a highly technical and graceful dance form that originated during the Italian Renaissance and blossomed in the royal courts of France. Over centuries, it evolved into a global art form, celebrated for its beauty, discipline, and emotional depth.

Classical ballet is characterized by its precise movements, structured choreography, pointe work, and storytelling through mime and motion. From the grandeur of Russian ballets like Swan Lake and The Nutcracker, to contemporary interpretations, ballet continues to evolve while maintaining its classical roots.
