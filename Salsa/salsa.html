About Salsa

Salsa is a lively, energetic partner dance that originated in the Caribbean â€” with strong influences from Cuban Son, mambo, cha-cha, and jazz. Known for its fast footwork, spins, body isolation, and syncopated rhythms, Salsa has become a global sensation.

The word "salsa" means "sauce" in Spanish, symbolizing the flavorful blend of cultures, rhythms, and movements. The dance is often improvisational, allowing dancers to showcase their style and musicality.

Whether performed socially or competitively, Salsa is a celebration of connection, music, and joy. From the streets of Havana to ballrooms and dance studios worldwide, its infectious energy continues to bring people together.
